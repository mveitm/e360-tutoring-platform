## 5. Executive summary

The external proposal says public Sales-Ready should mean Bexauri can be publicly available to buy without overpromising.

It is not:

* Closed sandbox.
* Private beta.
* Expanded pilot.
* Waitlist.
* Internal validation.
* M1-only capability.

The candidate gate proposes that public Sales-Ready would require Bexauri to sustain a minimally effective, autonomous, subscription-aligned PAES preparation experience across the three initial tutorias:

* PAES M1.
* PAES M2.
* Competencia Lectora L1.

Public Sales-Ready remains blocked today. This phase only versions the proposal.

## 6. Governing principle

Candidate principle:

Bexauri should be public Sales-Ready only when it can sustain for a paying student an indefinite, autonomous, and minimally effective PAES preparation experience in M1, M2, and L1, with successive pertinent loads, enough feedback to learn, progress evidence, and operation without ordinary human intervention.

Clarifications:

* "Indefinite operation" means no ordinary functional wall while subscription is active. It does not mean infinite content magic or unlimited guaranteed content quality.
* "Zero human intervention" means zero ordinary intervention in the learning trajectory. It does not mean no human supervision, support, audit, quality review, or exception handling.
* "Students improve" must be treated as an internal evidence gate, not as a public promise.

## 7. Public Sales-Ready definition

Candidate definition:

Public Sales-Ready would mean Bexauri can be publicly offered and bought with a promise that matches actual product behavior.

At minimum, the student should be able to:

* Register.
* Enroll in one or more tutorias.
* Pay or have an active subscription according to a future approved flow.
* Access the app.
* See enrolled tutorias in homepage/dashboard.
* See progress per tutoria.
* Receive the next StudyLoad.
* Complete questions.
* Receive brief feedback per question.
* Open complete feedback per question.
* Understand step-by-step reasoning.
* Receive recommendations for similar exercises.
* Continue with successive loads without ordinary pause.
* Return to the app and resume.
* Progress without ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory.

This definition is a proposal. It does not approve public Sales-Ready.

## 8. Gate by area: M1

Candidate M1 gate would require:

* Enrollment in M1.
* Successive M1 loads.
* No ordinary functional limit while subscription is active.
* Logical and pertinent progression.
* Brief feedback per question.
* Complete feedback per question.
* Homepage/dashboard progress evidence.
* Response to deficits through pertinent future loads.
* No ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory.

M1 candidate NO-GO if:

* M1 is only a short route.
* M1 is a demo.
* M1 is a finite sequence with no continuity plan.
* M1 feedback is superficial.
* M1 requires ordinary human intervention for the student to continue.

## 9. Gate by area: M2

Candidate M2 gate would require:

* Its own pedagogical identity.
* Not a duplicated M1 route.
* Not a nominal label.
* Enrollment in M2.
* Successive M2 loads.
* No ordinary functional limit while subscription is active.
* M2-specific competencies and difficulty.
* Logical progression.
* Brief and complete feedback.
* Homepage/dashboard progress evidence.
* No ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory.

M2 candidate NO-GO if:

* It is nominal only.
* It has no route.
* It has no continuity.
* It requires ordinary human intervention for progress.

## 10. Gate by area: L1

Candidate L1 gate would require:

* Specific focus on reading competence.
* Appropriate texts.
* Questions aligned to reading objectives.
* Feedback that explains correctness and reading errors.
* Coverage of skills such as inference, purpose, contextual vocabulary, or other reading competencies.
* Brief feedback.
* Complete feedback.
* Homepage/dashboard progress evidence.
* No ordinary functional wall while subscription is active.
* No ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory.

L1 candidate NO-GO if:

* It is a generic quiz.
* Texts are inadequate.
* Pedagogical feedback is insufficient.
* There is no continuity.

## 11. Minimum student experience

Candidate minimum student experience:

```text
I know what to do, I receive enough help to learn from my mistakes, and there is always a next step while my subscription is active.
```

This statement is an internal experience target, not public copy.

The student experience would require:

* Clear registration/onboarding.
* Clear tutoria enrollment.
* Clear first concrete action.
* Calm dashboard/homepage orientation.
* Feedback that supports learning.
* Continuity without ordinary human release.
* No exposure to internal admin states.
* No unsupported PAES score or outcome promise.

## 12. Onboarding and registration boundary

Candidate public Sales-Ready onboarding would require:

* Sober explanation of Bexauri.
* M1, M2, and L1 presented as available tutorias only if actually operational.
* Clear enrollment.
* Clear distinction between registration, access, and subscription.
* Route to the first concrete action.
* No score promises.
* No complete PAES promise.
* No internal state exposure.

This phase does not change signup, onboarding, copy, access, or UI.

## 13. In-app continuity requirement

Candidate continuity requirement:

* Successive loads in each enrolled tutoria.
* No ordinary waiting for human intervention in the learning trajectory.
* No premature functional route end while subscription is active.
* No ordinary manual assignment as the normal path.
* Return-and-resume behavior.
* Clear next step or clear stop state.

"No ordinary functional wall" means the app should not strand the paying student at a dead end under the promised scope. It does not mean unlimited or magical content.

## 14. Learning evidence and two-state feedback

Candidate learning evidence should include:

* Enrolled tutorias.
* Completed loads.
* Loads in progress.
* Pending or next loads.
* Performance.
* Progress per tutoria.
* Difficulty areas.
* Study continuity.
* Feedback available/received.
* Internal improvement signals.

Student improvement must be framed as internal evidence, not as a public score or outcome promise.

Two-state feedback proposal:

Brief feedback should be:

* Visible directly on screen.
* Short.
* Clear.
* Useful.
* Explicit about right/wrong status.
* Explicit about central idea.
* Explicit about the error to review.
* Clear about why complete explanation may help.

Complete feedback should be:

* Optional and accessible.
* Step-by-step.
* Clear about why the correct alternative is correct.
* Clear about the reasoning path.
* Connected to recommendations for similar exercises.
* Written in student-friendly language.
* Useful for integration and review.

## 15. Sustainable human supervision

Candidate Sales-Ready posture:

* The student uses the app.
* The app gives ordinary continuity.
* The human supervises, audits functionality, reviews pedagogical quality globally, handles exceptions, and reports issues into change control.

Candidate NO-GO:

* If a human must touch the ordinary trajectory so students can advance, public Sales-Ready should remain blocked.

This does not remove human supervision, support, or audit. It only rejects human intervention as the ordinary path for student continuity.

## 16. Admin/tutor operation requirements

Candidate admin/tutor operation would require visibility into:

* Active students.
* Tutorias enrolled.
* Progress by tutoria.
* Loads completed.
* Loads in progress.
* Pending/next loads.
* Performance evidence.
* General problems.
* Continuity errors.
* Minimal pedagogical signals.

Admin/tutor operation should not depend on:

* DB inspection.
* Scripts.
* Technical intervention.
* Logs as the normal view.
* Individual contact to know whether a student advanced.

## 17. Payment/subscription readiness boundary

Public Sales-Ready would require future payment/subscription operational readiness, but this phase does not decide:

* Price.
* Payment provider.
* Legal terms.
* Minor policy.
* Refund/pause terms.
* Subscription scope.
* Billing source of truth.

Minimum future criteria should include:

* Student understands what is bought.
* Subscription links coherently to access.
* While paying, student can use enrolled tutorias under the promised scope.
* App sustains no ordinary functional wall in M1/M2/L1 while subscription is active.
* Expiration, cancellation, and pause are clear.
* No charge for what the app cannot deliver.
* Compact terms and conditions exist before public sale.

Payment/minor policy remains unresolved and Pro/high-level reserved.

## 18. Commercial promise limits

Candidate allowed public promise, if future gate approves:

* Support for PAES M1/M2/L1 competencies.
* Progressive study loads.
* Feedback by question.
* Study continuity.
* Minimal progress reports.
* Guided and supervised experience.

Do not promise:

* Guaranteed score.
* Guaranteed score increase.
* Complete PAES preparation.
* Advanced AI tutor if not implemented.
* Sophisticated cognitive adaptation.
* Teacher replacement.
* Fast results.
* Learning without effort.
* Personalization not implemented.

## 19. Absolute NO-GO conditions

Candidate public Sales-Ready NO-GO if:

* App has critical errors.
* App cannot sustain no ordinary functional wall in M1, M2, and L1.
* Student cannot receive successive loads without ordinary pause while paying.
* Brief and complete feedback per question do not exist.
* Complete feedback does not support step-by-step understanding.
* Homepage/dashboard does not show progress for enrolled tutorias.
* Evidence with three test students does not exist.
* Tutorias do not show pertinent progression of loads.
* Internal evidence does not show students can integrate feedback.
* Internal evidence does not show improvement signals where students struggled.
* Human intervention is needed in the ordinary learning trajectory.
* M2 or L1 are nominal.
* Payment/subscription promises more than the app delivers.
* Public copy overpromises.
* Compact terms and conditions are missing.

These are candidate NO-GO conditions. This phase does not make a final Sales-Ready decision.

## 20. Beta-public vs Sales-Ready distinction

The external proposal notes that a limited public beta may exist before Sales-Ready.

Candidate boundary:

* A limited public beta must not be sold or framed as Sales-Ready.
* It may be considered only if future explicit direction accepts that it is not public sale.
* It would require sandbox evidence, support definition, route/content readiness, two-state feedback, dashboard progress, and closed payment testing if payment is involved.

This phase does not create public beta, public sale, copy approval, or implementation.

## 21. Pause / rollback / return-to-sandbox signals

Candidate pause, stop, rollback, or return-to-sandbox signals:

* Students do not understand what to do.
* Continuity breaks.
* Ordinary human intervention is required in the learning trajectory.
* Feedback does not help learning.
* M2/L1 are nominal.
* Payment/access is confusing.
* Dashboard lacks progress.
* Critical errors appear.
* Students report frustration or no value.
* Copy creates wrong expectations.
* Support cannot respond.
* Human work sustains the ordinary trajectory.

These signals would require future review before any public expansion.

## 22. Critical risks: brand, pedagogy, operations, technical

| Risk area | Candidate risk | Candidate mitigation |

| Brand | Public offer overpromises M1/M2/L1 readiness. | Keep public Sales-Ready blocked until evidence exists. |
| Pedagogy | M2/L1 are nominal or shallow. | Require area-specific identity, route, and feedback. |
| Operations | Human becomes ordinary trajectory operator. | Require no ordinary human intervention in trajectory. |
| Technical | Continuity depends on scripts, logs, DB, or manual setup. | Require app/admin visibility and ordinary app continuity. |
| Payment | Subscription promises more than the app delivers. | Payment/subscription readiness would require separate Pro/high-level decisions. |
| Minors | Minor payment and consent are unresolved. | Keep payment/minor policy Pro/high-level reserved. |
| Copy | Public copy creates score/complete-PAES expectation. | Require sober copy and explicit no-overpromise review. |

## 23. Minimum evidence before public opening

Candidate evidence package before public opening would include:

* Use of M1/M2/L1 according to defined scope.
* Pertinent progression of loads in each tutoria.
* Use of brief and complete feedback.
* Student report of integrating feedback.
* Internal improvement signals in exercises/loads where difficulty appeared.
* Continuity without ordinary pause.
* Zero ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory.
* No critical errors.
* Enrollment by tutoria.
* Dashboard progress per tutoria.
* Successive loads without ordinary functional wall.
* Admin/tutor visibility.
* Payment/subscription tested in closed sandbox if payment is involved.
* Access coherent with subscription if payment is involved.
* Expiration/cancel/pause understandable.
* Basic support defined.
* Compact terms and conditions prepared.
* Public copy reviewed and sober.

This is candidate evidence, not public claim approval.

## 24. Final decision checklist

A future final Sales-Ready decision should ask:

* Is M1 operational beyond a short demo?
* Is M2 operational as M2, not a label?
* Is L1 operational as reading competence, not generic quiz?
* Does each tutoria have successive pertinent loads?
* Does each tutoria provide brief and complete feedback?
* Does the dashboard/homepage show progress per enrolled tutoria?
* Can the student continue without ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory?
* Can admin/tutor observe progress without DB/scripts/logs as normal work?
* Is payment/subscription source of truth defined?
* Are expiration/cancel/pause understandable?
* Are minor/payment questions resolved?
* Is public copy sober and non-overpromising?
* Is support capacity defined?
* Is public Sales-Ready still blocked by any absolute NO-GO?

This checklist is proposed for future use. It does not approve Sales-Ready now.
