# MVP-COMMERCIAL-PAES-M2-ALIGNMENT-1 - Controlled Official-Source Alignment Map

## 1. Phase

* Phase: `MVP-COMMERCIAL-PAES-M2-ALIGNMENT-1`.
* Type: documentation-only / controlled M2 official-source alignment / candidate route-source mapping.
* Product horizon: roadmap change-control / M2 official-source alignment / Sales-Ready support.
* This is not `MVP-Beta-Sales-Ready` approval.
* M2 remains not ready.
* L1 remains not ready.
* Public Sales-Ready remains blocked.

## 2. Relationship to SOURCE-1C and M2-READINESS-1

`MVP-COMMERCIAL-PAES-SOURCE-1C` created a controlled, copyright-safe high-level source-content summary for M2 and L1.

`MVP-COMMERCIAL-M2-READINESS-1` versioned a non-binding candidate M2 route/content preparation proposal.

This document maps the M2-READINESS-1 candidate M2 route and StudyLoad families against the high-level official M2 source summaries already extracted in SOURCE-1C.

It does not re-extract official sources. It does not replace SOURCE-1C. It does not validate the M2 proposal as final.

## 3. Nature of document

This is a controlled alignment map.

It is:

* documentation-only;
* high-level;
* source-summary based;
* conservative;
* intended to support later M2 taxonomy and route-readiness phases.

It is not:

* full source extraction;
* official table reproduction;
* final M2 taxonomy;
* M2 content design;
* StudyLoad design;
* question design;
* feedback design;
* implementation authority;
* M2 readiness approval;
* Sales-Ready approval.

## 4. Purpose

The purpose is to identify where the candidate M2 proposal appears compatible with the high-level official M2 source summary and where it remains too broad, vague, or unsupported for taxonomy or implementation.

This phase helps future work decide:

* which candidate route families have high-level source fit;
* which candidate route families need deeper source-content extraction;
* which candidate StudyLoad families are only conceptual;
* which risks must remain reserved for Pro/high-level direction;
* which future phases must happen before M2 taxonomy, content, feedback, dashboard evidence, or readiness can be considered.

## 7. Alignment method

The mapping method is intentionally limited:

1. Start from the high-level SOURCE-1C M2 skill families and knowledge domains.
2. Compare each candidate M2 route family to those high-level categories only.
3. Mark high-level source fit using conservative language.
4. Identify gaps, overreach risks, and deeper extraction needs.
5. Preserve a hard boundary between candidate alignment and final taxonomy.

Alignment language means only candidate compatibility at a high level. It does not mean the proposal is officially aligned, complete, implementation-ready, or ready for students.

## 12. Aligned areas

The candidate proposal appears compatible at a high level in these areas:

* algebra/function-oriented families map naturally to the high-level domain `algebra and functions`;
* functions and graphs have an evident relationship with `representation`;
* mathematical modeling has an evident relationship with the high-level skill family `modeling`;
* geometry-oriented reasoning has a plausible relationship with the high-level domain `geometry`;
* integrated problems may be useful later if they are explicitly constrained by source-aligned domains and skills.

These are candidate alignment observations only. They do not establish official alignment, content readiness, or taxonomy completeness.

## 13. Gaps and weakly supported areas

Current gaps:

* `numbers` is present in SOURCE-1C but not clearly represented as a candidate route family except indirectly through prerequisite or integrated work.
* `probability and statistics` is present in SOURCE-1C but not clearly represented as a candidate route family.
* `argumentation` is present in SOURCE-1C but the candidate proposal does not yet define how M2 students would justify reasoning or evaluate solution validity.
* `integrated problems` is too broad without source-content detail and taxonomy criteria.
* `diagnostic / prerequisite check` is not source-defined yet and needs product/pedagogical direction before being treated as a route component.
* Candidate StudyLoad families are conceptual and do not include source-backed objectives, item criteria, feedback boundaries, or dashboard evidence.

## 14. Overreach risks

Risks:

* Treating this map as final official M2 alignment.
* Treating high-level SOURCE-1C summaries as enough to define a detailed taxonomy.
* Treating `integrated problems` as a catch-all for unsupported content.
* Treating `mathematical modeling` as any word problem rather than a source-aligned skill family.
* Treating M2 as M1 with harder algebra.
* Creating M2 StudyLoads before source-content extraction, taxonomy, solution validation, and feedback design.
* Declaring M2 ready because high-level categories appear compatible.
* Using dashboard/progress or feedback language before M2 content exists.

## 16. M2 taxonomy boundary

This document does not create a M2 taxonomy.

A future M2 taxonomy phase must be separate and must depend on:

* accepted source-content extraction detail;
* an approved mapping method;
* explicit inclusion/exclusion criteria;
* separation from M1;
* Pro/high-level direction on granularity and readiness threshold;
* later content, feedback, dashboard, and app/student evidence phases.

The alignment map is an input to later taxonomy work, not the taxonomy itself.

## 17. Future M2 source-content needs

Future M2 source-content work should:

* preserve SOURCE-1C-M2 hierarchy and HTML body caveat;
* extract only necessary official structure in a copyright-safe way;
* avoid copying official tables;
* identify official source detail needed to distinguish M2 from M1;
* clarify whether numbers and probability/statistics require explicit candidate route families;
* clarify how argumentation appears in source expectations;
* define which official details are safe to store and paraphrase.

## 18. Future M2 route taxonomy prerequisites

Before a M2 route taxonomy phase:

* source-content detail must be sufficient to support route boundaries;
* candidate route families must be reviewed for coverage gaps;
* `integrated problems` must be constrained;
* M2 must be separated from M1 by content identity and reasoning expectations;
* prerequisite/diagnostic logic must be scoped;
* initial route depth and breadth must be reserved for Pro/high-level direction.

## 19. Future M2 feedback/dashboard implications

Future feedback implications:

* M2 feedback must not recycle M1 explanations under an M2 label.
* Feedback should later reflect M2 reasoning, representation, modeling, and justification where supported.
* Error diagnosis families need future item-level and feedback-level design.

Future dashboard implications:

* M2 progress must be separate from M1 progress.
* Dashboard evidence must rely on real M2 activity, not labels.
* Progress categories must not imply PAES score, theta, mastery, ranking, or complete preparation.
* App/student evidence requires separate implementation and validation phases.

## 20. PASS / FAIL / BLOCKED interpretation

Documentation PASS for this phase means:

* the candidate M2 proposal has been mapped conservatively to SOURCE-1C high-level categories;
* gaps and overreach risks are documented;
* taxonomy, content, StudyLoad, feedback, dashboard, implementation, readiness, and Sales-Ready boundaries remain intact.

This phase would FAIL if it:

* creates final taxonomy;
* creates M2 content;
* creates StudyLoads;
* creates registry entries;
* copies official tables;
* declares M2 ready;
* approves Sales-Ready.

Future M2 readiness remains BLOCKED until separate phases produce source-aligned taxonomy, content, feedback, dashboard evidence, app/student evidence, and required governance approvals.

## 21. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High-level mapping becomes hidden taxonomy | Would bypass source detail and Pro/high-level direction | State taxonomy boundary and require future taxonomy phase |
| Source summaries are overread | SOURCE-1C intentionally avoids detailed tables | Use only high-level skill/domain labels and mark gaps |
| Candidate StudyLoad labels become implementation scope | Could create registry or content before readiness | Label all StudyLoad families as conceptual only |
| M2 remains nominal | Public Sales-Ready gate requires real M2 identity | Require future source-aligned taxonomy, M2-specific feedback, and app evidence |
| Numbers and probability/statistics are underrepresented | Coverage may be too narrow | Flag as future source-content and route taxonomy needs |
| Integrated problems become a vague bucket | Weakens route quality and evidence | Require constraints before taxonomy or content |

## 22. Questions reserved for Pro/high-level direction

Reserved questions:

* Exact M2 taxonomy granularity.
* Whether numbers and probability/statistics need explicit initial route families.
* Whether prerequisite/diagnostic work should be visible, dynamic, or both.
* Minimum M2 coverage before controlled sandbox use.
* Minimum M2 coverage before any public Sales-Ready consideration.
* How to distinguish M2 from M1 without overbuilding.
* Whether integrated problems should be a late route family or only an assessment/review mode.
* Required pedagogical or expert review threshold before M2 content creation.
* Required app/student evidence before M2 can count toward a public offer.
