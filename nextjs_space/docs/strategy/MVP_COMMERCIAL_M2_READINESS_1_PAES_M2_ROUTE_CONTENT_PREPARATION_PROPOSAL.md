## 5. Executive summary

The external proposal argues that M2 cannot be a nominal extension of M1 or a copy of M1 with harder exercises.

A credible M2 line would need to demonstrate that Bexauri recognizes the pedagogical difference between M1 and M2. It should have:

* a distinct mathematical identity;
* candidate route/content families;
* M2-specific StudyLoads;
* feedback adapted to M2 reasoning;
* dashboard/progress evidence separated from M1;
* later official-source alignment;
* later pedagogical review;
* later content validation;
* later app/student evidence.

This phase versions the proposal only. It creates no M2 implementation.

## 6. Governing principle for M2

Candidate principle:

M2 should help the student build strategies for solving higher-abstraction mathematical problems, integrating representation, modeling, condition analysis, and justification of procedures.

Safe interpretation:

* M2 should be a distinct tutoria, not M1 renamed.
* M2 should require later official-source alignment before any readiness claim.
* M2 should require later pedagogical review before any sellable-offer claim.
* M2 should require later content validation and app/student evidence before any public Sales-Ready claim.

## 7. M1 vs M2 distinction

Candidate distinction:

* M1 is more associated with general mathematical competencies and broad application.
* M2 would require more mathematical depth, abstraction, symbolic manipulation, condition interpretation, and connections between representations.

This distinction is candidate framing. It is not official-source alignment.

M2 should not be:

* M1 with a new label;
* M1 with only harder numbers;
* a generic advanced math bucket;
* a dashboard card without route/content identity;
* content the system cannot explain safely.

## 8. Candidate M2 identity

Candidate M2 identity may include:

* problems not solved only by direct substitution;
* more functions, algebraic expressions, graphs, conditions, and parameters;
* emphasis on selecting a strategy before calculating;
* modeling and representation choices;
* condition and restriction analysis;
* feedback explaining why a path is valid or invalid;
* progress separated from M1;
* clear signs of deeper mathematical content.

This identity requires later official PAES M2 source/blueprint alignment.

## 9. Candidate M2 route map

Candidate route map:

1. M2 initial diagnostic / prerequisite check.
2. Algebra and expressions.
3. Functions and graphs.
4. Mathematical modeling.
5. Analytical geometry or advanced geometric reasoning.
6. Integrated problems.

Safe adjustment:

These are candidate route families only. They are not approved official M2 blocks, not final taxonomy, and not implementation scope.

## 10. Candidate M2 StudyLoad families

Candidate StudyLoad families, using Spanish labels as provisional conceptual names:

* Calentamiento de prerrequisitos algebraicos M2.
* Interpretacion de funciones M2.
* Modelacion de problemas M2.
* Diagnostico de errores conceptuales M2.
* Conjunto mixto de razonamiento M2.
* Repaso M2 despues de errores.

These names are not final StudyLoad titles.

They do not create content registry entries, routes, activities, exercises, feedback, or UI.

## 11. M2 feedback expectations

Aligned with Gate 1B, M2 feedback would need its own reasoning depth.

Candidate feedback breve should indicate:

* answer state;
* likely error type;
* useful hint;
* what to review, such as condition, formula, graph, equivalence, domain, calculation, or strategy.

Candidate feedback completo should include:

* step-by-step reasoning;
* strategy justification;
* common error warnings;
* restriction/condition review;
* distractor reasoning when useful;
* connection to the M2 skill.

M2 feedback should not recycle M1 explanations under an M2 label.

## 12. M2 dashboard/progress expectations

Aligned with Gate 1C, M2 dashboard/progress evidence would need to show M2 separately from M1.

Candidate M2 dashboard/progress evidence may include:

* M2 enrollment state;
* current or next M2 StudyLoad;
* completed M2 loads;
* M2 skills or families;
* latest M2 activity;
* strengths and weaknesses when supported by actual M2 evidence;
* recurring M2 error families;
* depth progression;
* feedback availability for M2 work.

Dashboard evidence must not mix M1 and M2 progress or imply M2 readiness without M2 route/content support.

## 13. Minimum evidence that M2 is not nominal

Candidate minimum evidence that M2 is not nominal:

* M2 taxonomy separate from M1.
* M2 StudyLoads with their own objectives.
* Questions that are not M1 copies.
* Feedback breve and feedback completo adapted to M2 reasoning.
* Dashboard/progress evidence with differentiated M2 progress.
* Performance evidence by M2 skill or family.
* Pedagogical review confirming M2 relevance.
* Student/tutor validation before considering M2 part of a sellable offer.

This evidence does not exist because of this document. It would require future content, implementation, review, and validation phases.

## 14. Risks of cloning M1 as M2

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weak or misleading pedagogical promise | Students may believe Bexauri supports M2 when it only relabels M1. | Require M2 identity, route map, and source alignment. |
| Low value for advanced students | M2 students may quickly detect shallow content. | Require depth, modeling, strategy, and condition analysis. |
| Inflated dashboard progress | Progress could look complete without M2 evidence. | Separate M2 dashboard/progress from M1. |
| Insufficient feedback for conceptual errors | Complex M2 errors need more reasoning support. | Require M2-specific brief and complete feedback. |
| Students needing M2 are badly served | Brand and pedagogical trust risk. | Keep M2 not ready until evidence exists. |
| Sales-Ready gate weakened | Public M1/M2/L1 gate becomes a label exercise. | Keep public Sales-Ready blocked if M2 is nominal. |

## 15. M2 NO-GO boundaries

M2 remains `NO-GO` for readiness if:

* content is unrevised;
* advanced problems lack validated solutions;
* exercises cannot be explained safely by the system;
* generic feedback is reused from M1;
* dashboard mixes M1 and M2 progress;
* M2 route is only a label;
* complete M2 simulations are promised without enough coverage;
* public copy promises complete M2 preparation;
* complex adaptive routing is claimed without evidence;
* high-difficulty content has explanation errors that could damage trust.
