## 3. Nature of document

This is a candidate criterion document.

It is not:

* an evidence record;
* a live test report;
* a market validation report;
* a pricing validation report;
* a student learning outcome validation report;
* a public Sales-Ready decision.

Public Sales-Ready remains blocked. M1-only remains not Sales-Ready.

## 4. Purpose

The purpose is to define what evidence would need to exist from three controlled test students before Bexauri can even consider a future public Sales-Ready decision.

The package should help determine whether Bexauri can sustain the future public promise across:

* PAES M1;
* PAES M2;
* Competencia Lectora L1;
* continuity without an ordinary functional wall;
* two-state feedback;
* dashboard/homepage progress evidence;
* no ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory;
* student perception of whether feedback helped learning;
* internal improvement signals where students struggled;
* admin/tutor visibility;
* critical error, blocker, and support burden evidence.

Evidence with three students would be necessary but not sufficient for public Sales-Ready.

## 5. Definition of three-student evidence package

The three-student evidence package is a future internal evidence package based on controlled test students.

It is designed to check whether Bexauri can sustain the future Sales-Ready promise in a controlled setting before any public sale.

It should require evidence that each relevant test student can:

* access the app under a controlled future test condition;
* work in the intended tutoria or tutorias;
* complete successive StudyLoads;
* receive feedback breve and feedback completo;
* see dashboard/homepage progress evidence when implemented;
* continue without ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory;
* report whether feedback helped them understand mistakes or next steps;
* show internal improvement signals where difficulty appeared;
* avoid critical blockers.

Evidence should be classified as:

* Required.
* Optional but useful.
* Not allowed as public claim.
* Blocker if missing.

## 6. What the three-student evidence package does not mean

Three-student evidence does not mean:

* market validation;
* public pricing validation;
* product-market fit;
* guaranteed learning outcomes;
* public sale authorization;
* public beta authorization;
* public Sales-Ready approval;
* complete PAES preparation;
* proof that all future students will succeed;
* replacement for M1/M2/L1 route/content validation;
* replacement for payment, legal, minor, consent, or subscription review.

Future evidence collection requires separate phases and actual app/student validation.

This phase does not collect evidence, contact students, operate the app, validate M1/M2/L1, validate payment/subscription, approve public launch, or validate actual student learning value.

## 7. Participant classes and privacy boundary

The future package should use controlled test students only.

Participant handling should follow these boundaries:

* Use privacy-safe labels or redacted identifiers in general documentation.
* Avoid unnecessary personal data.
* Avoid sensitive minor/payment details in general docs.
* Treat any minor participation as Pro/high-level reserved if payment, consent, guardian communication, or legal/ethical handling is involved.
* Keep participant evidence internal unless a later explicit review authorizes a sanitized summary.
* Do not treat three controlled students as a public market sample.

Candidate participant classes:

| Participant class | Candidate use in evidence package | Payment/minor boundary | Public claim boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Owner/operator | Can support internal rehearsal only; not counted as student evidence for public sale | No payment; no minor issue | Not market or student validation |
| Known adult test student | Candidate controlled evidence source in a future explicit phase | Payment still requires separate review | Internal evidence only |
| Known minor test student | Candidate only after guardian/consent/legal/ethical handling is resolved | Pro/high-level reserved | Internal evidence only |
| Unknown external student | Not appropriate for the initial three-student package without a separate gate | Pro/high-level reserved | Risks public-offer drift |

## 9. M1 evidence requirements

M1 evidence would require:

* real M1 enrollment or controlled equivalent in a future authorized test;
* successive M1 StudyLoads, not a one-screen demo;
* M1-appropriate progression and difficulty;
* feedback breve and feedback completo on M1 questions;
* student-visible M1 progress evidence when dashboard/homepage progress is implemented;
* return/resume continuity;
* no ordinary human intervention to keep the M1 trajectory moving;
* internal improvement signals where the student struggled;
* admin/tutor visibility into M1 activity, progress, friction, and support burden.

M1 may have partial precedent, but M1 evidence alone cannot establish public Sales-Ready because the future gate is M1/M2/L1.

## 10. M2 evidence requirements

M2 evidence would require:

* a real M2 route/content identity, not M1 renamed or duplicated under another label;
* M2-specific StudyLoads and difficulty expectations;
* successive M2 loads;
* M2-specific feedback breve and feedback completo;
* student-visible M2 progress evidence when dashboard/homepage progress is implemented;
* continuity without ordinary human intervention;
* internal signals that the student can use M2 feedback to continue;
* admin/tutor visibility into M2 progress and blockers.

M2 evidence is `BLOCKED` if M2 is nominal, lacks route/content identity, lacks continuity, or depends on manual human assignment as the normal path.

## 11. L1 evidence requirements

L1 evidence would require:

* a real reading-competence route/content identity;
* appropriate texts and reading questions;
* evidence tied to reading skills such as inference, purpose, contextual vocabulary, structure, evidence in the text, or other relevant L1 skills;
* feedback breve and feedback completo that explain reading errors, not generic answer-key feedback;
* student-visible L1 progress evidence when dashboard/homepage progress is implemented;
* continuity without ordinary human intervention;
* internal signals that the student can use feedback to improve a reading task or understand a reading mistake;
* admin/tutor visibility into L1 activity, progress, and blockers.

L1 evidence is `BLOCKED` if L1 is a generic quiz, lacks appropriate texts, lacks reading-specific feedback, or lacks continuity.

## 12. Continuity evidence requirements

Continuity evidence should show:

* the student receives a next StudyLoad or clear next action;
* the student can return after a break and understand where to resume;
* no ordinary functional wall appears while the controlled scope remains active;
* any stop state is clear, intentional, and not caused by missing work;
* support is used for exceptions, not ordinary trajectory continuation;
* continuity works separately for M1, M2, and L1 where each tutoria is in scope.

Continuity evidence does not mean infinite content magic. It means no ordinary functional wall inside the promised scope while the future subscription or controlled test condition is active.

## 13. Feedback-use evidence requirements

Feedback-use evidence should show:

* the student saw feedback breve;
* the student could access feedback completo;
* the student understood why the complete explanation was useful;
* the student could describe at least one mistake or reasoning step after feedback;
* feedback helped the student choose a next action or review target;
* feedback stayed student-safe, brief where needed, and not overwhelming.

Feedback-use evidence is insufficient if it only records that feedback was generated. The package should show that feedback was visible, understandable, and usable by the student.

## 14. Dashboard/progress evidence requirements

Dashboard/homepage progress evidence should show, when implemented:

* enrolled tutorias;
* active/inactive state per tutoria where safe;
* current or next StudyLoad;
* completed loads;
* in-progress loads;
* latest activity or continuity signal;
* basic performance summary where safe;
* feedback availability;
* simple focus/review areas only when supported by actual activity evidence;
* no PAES score prediction, theta, mastery, ranking, or unsupported long-term projection.

Dashboard evidence is not a public proof of learning. It is a future readiness signal that the student can understand current state and next step.

## 15. No-human-intervention trajectory evidence

The package should distinguish:

* ordinary learning trajectory intervention;
* exception support;
* supervision;
* audit;
* technical troubleshooting.

For a future public Sales-Ready gate, the evidence should show zero ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory. That means the app, not the human, provides the normal next step.

This does not mean no supervision, support, or audit exists. It means human support is not the mechanism that keeps ordinary study moving.

Evidence should include:

* intervention count;
* intervention type;
* whether the intervention was avoidable;
* whether the student could continue afterward through the app;
* whether repeated intervention indicates product gap.

## 16. Student perception / self-report evidence

Student self-report should cover:

* whether the first step was clear;
* whether the student knew what to do next;
* whether feedback breve helped orient them;
* whether feedback completo helped explain the mistake or reasoning;
* whether progress evidence helped them understand current state;
* whether the experience felt limited, confusing, useful, frustrating, or sustainable;
* whether they expected a promise the app did not make.

Self-report matters, but it cannot substitute for actual app evidence, route/content readiness, feedback evidence, continuity, or admin/tutor visibility.

## 17. Improvement signal evidence

Improvement signals are internal evidence only. They must not become public guarantees.

Candidate internal signals:

* fewer repeated mistakes in a similar exercise after feedback;
* corrected misconception observed in a later response;
* improved completion rate in a target area;
* student uses feedback language to explain a later answer;
* performance stabilizes in a difficulty area after review;
* student can state what to review next.

Improvement signal evidence does not mean:

* guaranteed score increase;
* validated learning outcome for public claim;
* complete PAES readiness;
* proof of market value.

## 18. Admin/tutor visibility evidence

Admin/tutor visibility evidence should show that an operator can understand student progress without DB inspection, scripts, technical logs, or individual contact as the normal source of truth.

Evidence should include:

* active students;
* enrolled tutorias;
* progress by tutoria;
* completed/in-progress/pending loads;
* basic performance evidence;
* feedback availability/use where available;
* general problems or blockers;
* continuity errors;
* support burden;
* minimal pedagogical signals relevant to M1, M2, and L1.

Admin/tutor visibility is insufficient if the operator must inspect the database, run scripts, read logs, or manually ask each student to know whether the student advanced.

## 19. Critical error / blocker / support burden evidence

The package should record:

* critical app errors;
* route/content blockers;
* access blockers;
* feedback blockers;
* dashboard/progress blockers;
* continuity blockers;
* support requests;
* support time;
* repeated human interventions;
* unresolved defects;
* student frustration or confusion signals.

Public Sales-Ready should remain blocked if critical errors persist, blockers prevent normal continuity, support burden is high, or human intervention is required as the ordinary path.

## 20. Insufficient or misleading evidence

Evidence is insufficient or misleading if it relies on:

* owner/operator rehearsal instead of controlled student evidence;
* three known students as market validation;
* M1-only evidence for an M1/M2/L1 gate;
* M2 or L1 as nominal labels without real route/content identity;
* screenshots without continuity proof;
* self-report without app evidence;
* completion counts without feedback usefulness;
* dashboard numbers without a clear next step;
* improvement signals converted into public guarantees;
* human manually sustaining the learning trajectory;
* payment/access success without pedagogical evidence;
* absence of complaints as proof of readiness.

## 21. Evidence package PASS criteria

A future three-student evidence package may be considered `PASS` only if:

* participant records are privacy-safe;
* evidence covers the required M1/M2/L1 scope;
* M2 and L1 have real identity-specific evidence;
* students complete successive loads in the relevant tutorias;
* feedback breve and feedback completo are visible, understandable, and usable;
* dashboard/homepage progress evidence helps students understand current state and next step when implemented;
* students continue without ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory;
* self-report indicates feedback and next-step clarity are understandable;
* internal improvement signals exist where difficulty appeared;
* admin/tutor visibility works without DB/scripts/logs as the ordinary source;
* critical blockers are absent or resolved;
* support burden is sustainable;
* evidence is not used as market, pricing, or outcome guarantee.

Passing the evidence package would still be necessary but not sufficient for public Sales-Ready.

## 22. Evidence package FAIL criteria

The package should be `FAIL` if:

* M2 or L1 evidence is nominal;
* M1-only evidence is treated as enough;
* students cannot complete successive loads;
* feedback is absent, superficial, inaccessible, or not useful to students;
* progress evidence does not show current state or next step;
* ordinary human intervention is required to continue;
* critical blockers remain unresolved;
* admin/tutor visibility depends on DB inspection, scripts, or logs;
* support burden is high or undefined;
* self-report shows confusion about scope, next steps, or feedback;
* evidence is converted into public claims.

## 23. Evidence package BLOCKED criteria

The package should be `BLOCKED` if:

* evidence collection itself has not been authorized by a future phase;
* participant class, privacy handling, or minor/guardian policy is unresolved;
* route/content readiness for M2 or L1 is missing;
* dashboard/progress evidence is not implemented or cannot be observed;
* feedback breve/completo is not implemented or cannot be observed;
* no-human-intervention trajectory cannot be tested;
* payment, subscription, legal, or minor decisions are required to proceed;
* app operation, DB access, API calls, or runtime access changes are required but not authorized by a future phase;
* the package would imply public Sales-Ready approval.

## 24. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Three students treated as market validation | Creates false confidence and public-sale drift | Label as internal operational/pedagogical evidence only |
| Improvement signal becomes public promise | Creates unsupported outcome claim | Keep improvement signals internal and non-promissory |
| M1 evidence masks M2/L1 gaps | Breaks the M1/M2/L1 public gate | Require identity-specific M2 and L1 evidence |
| Human support hides product gaps | Makes readiness look stronger than app reality | Track intervention count and classify ordinary vs exception support |
| Self-report replaces app evidence | Weakens readiness proof | Require app, dashboard, feedback, continuity, and admin visibility evidence |
| Privacy overcollection | Raises ethical/legal risk | Use minimal, redacted, non-sensitive evidence |
| Minor/payment handling leaks into evidence phase | Requires Pro/high-level decisions | Keep payment/minor decisions separate and NO-GO until reviewed |
| Evidence used as public marketing | Overpromises readiness | Keep package internal unless a future explicit copy/legal review allows sanitized use |

* Whether all three students must test all M1/M2/L1 or whether coverage can be distributed.
* Minimum number of loads per tutoria.
* Definition of sufficient M2 identity.
* Definition of sufficient L1 reading-specific evidence.
* Minimum improvement signal threshold.
* Acceptable support burden.
* Acceptable technical error margin.
* Whether payment/subscription must be tested before public sale and under what policy.
* How evidence may be summarized without creating public promises.
