## 7. Authoring rules used

Feedback breve rules:

* short;
* student-safe;
* tied to locating explicit information;
* points to the relevant detail without long quotation;
* avoids shame, blame, score, mastery, ranking, theta, PAES prediction, guaranteed improvement, complete-preparation claims, and AI tutor claims.

Feedback completo rules:

* explains why the correct option is supported by one explicit detail;
* explains why each distractor fails for a clear textual reason;
* remains tied to locating information;
* avoids inference beyond the text;
* avoids overdiagnosing the student;
* avoids long quotation;
* stays concise enough for early L1;
* remains student-safe and non-punitive.

## 11. Feedback breve review

## 12. Feedback completo review

The three feedback completo entries:

* avoid long quotation;
* are concise enough for an early L1 pilot review path;
* are not approved for student or product use.

## 13. Evidence-reference review

Evidence references use short pointing and paraphrase rather than long quotation.

They refer only to:

* the explicit start time for item 1;
* the explicit arrival item for revised item 2;
* the explicit storage location for item 3.

They do not introduce outside facts, official PAES/DEMRE text, copyrighted text, or PAES-equivalence claims.

## 14. Distractor-explanation review

Distractor explanations are authored for future review only.

They explain why alternatives fail by distinguishing:

* condition time, end time, and day reference from the start time;
* sorted materials and containers from the arrival item;
* container, backpack table, and activity place from the storage location for leftover materials.

They do not diagnose the student, create new content, or become a StudyLoad or registry artifact.

## 19. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Authored feedback is mistaken for product approval | Would bypass feedback review, StudyLoad, registry, implementation, and evidence gates | Keep all student/product approval fields as no and label feedback as authored for future review only |
| Feedback becomes too broad or diagnostic | Could create unsafe student-facing claims | Keep explanations tied to answer/text relationships only |
| Evidence references overquote the draft text | Could create poor style and future rights drift | Use short pointing/paraphrase rather than long quotation |
| Distractor explanations create new teaching content | Would exceed locating-information scope | Keep explanations limited to why each alternative does not answer the question |
| L1 readiness is inferred from one text, three items, and feedback | Would weaken Sales-Ready gates | Keep L1 not ready and public Sales-Ready blocked |
