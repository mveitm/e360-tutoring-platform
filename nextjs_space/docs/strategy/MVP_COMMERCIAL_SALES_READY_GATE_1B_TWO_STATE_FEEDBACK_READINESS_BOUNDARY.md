This phase defines:

* feedback breve criteria;
* feedback completo criteria;
* shared cross-tutoria feedback criteria;

## 5. Definition of two-state feedback

Candidate definition:

Two-state feedback should mean that each evaluated question or exercise has two distinct levels of learning support:

1. Feedback breve: short, direct, student-safe feedback visible in the activity flow.
2. Feedback completo: optional, accessible, deeper explanation that supports reasoning and integration.

The two states should work together:

* feedback breve helps the student understand the immediate result without overload;
* feedback completo helps the student understand the reasoning path and learn from the error or confirmation;
* the student can continue without ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory;
* the app does not need to claim an AI tutor exists to provide this feedback.

## 6. What two-state feedback does not mean

Two-state feedback does not mean:

* an AI tutor exists;
* complete personalization exists;
* adaptive AI, theta, mastery, or advanced cognitive modeling exists;
* complete PAES preparation exists;
* improvement is guaranteed;
* every item is expert-validated;
* public copy is approved;
* UI is implemented;
* M1, M2, or L1 are public Sales-Ready;
* payment/subscription readiness exists;
* student learning value has been validated.

The safe interpretation is narrower: the future public Sales-Ready gate would require enough question-level feedback for a student to learn from answers without being stranded or dependent on ordinary human explanation.

## 7. Feedback breve criteria

Candidate feedback breve should be:

* visible directly in the activity flow;
* short;
* clear;
* student-safe;
* calm and non-punitive;
* specific to the question or answer;
* explicit about correct/incorrect status or an equivalent answer state;
* explicit about the central idea;
* explicit about the key error, misconception, or concept to review when relevant;
* clear about when and why the complete explanation is useful;
* brief enough not to interrupt study momentum;
* free of grandiose AI or outcome claims.

Feedback breve should not:

* shame the student;
* merely say "correct" or "incorrect" with no learning signal;
* expose internal scoring or admin state;
* pretend to infer more than the evidence supports;
* replace feedback completo when reasoning support is needed.

## 8. Feedback completo criteria

Candidate feedback completo should be:

* optional and accessible;
* step-by-step;
* clear about why the correct answer is correct;
* clear about the reasoning path;
* clear about common distractors or relevant wrong alternatives when useful;
* connected to the question, text, data, or mathematical object being evaluated;
* written in student-friendly language;
* useful for integrating the learning;
* connected to recommendations for similar exercises where appropriate;
* calm and non-punitive;
* free of unsupported AI tutor, complete personalization, or guaranteed learning claims.

Feedback completo should not:

* be a generic answer key;
* be so long that it overwhelms normal study;
* assume expert validation unless evidence exists;
* create a complete PAES promise;
* require ordinary human explanation for the student to continue.

## 9. Shared cross-tutoria criteria

Across M1, M2, and L1, a future public Sales-Ready gate would likely require:

* feedback breve and feedback completo for evaluated questions inside the promised scope;
* feedback language tied to the tutoria and skill being practiced;
* consistent availability, not one-off examples;
* student-safe tone;
* no unsupported outcome promise;
* support for error review;
* support for correct-answer confirmation;
* connection to future practice or similar exercises when useful;
* evidence that students can use feedback without ordinary human explanation;
* admin/tutor visibility into whether feedback exists or was used, if later required by dashboard/admin evidence phases.

## 10. M1 feedback readiness considerations

M1 may have partial feedback precedent, but this cannot be treated as public Sales-Ready.

Candidate M1 feedback readiness would require:

* feedback breve and completo for M1 mathematical questions inside the promised scope;
* explanation of mathematical reasoning, not only answer correctness;
* clear handling of common algebraic, functional, data, geometry, or numeric errors as applicable to the route;
* consistency across successive M1 loads;
* student-friendly language that helps the student review the relevant concept;
* evidence that feedback can support continuation without ordinary human intervention.

M1 candidate NO-GO if:

* feedback is only a short answer key;
* feedback is inconsistent across loads;
* feedback does not explain reasoning;
* feedback exists only in a demo fragment;
* human explanation is normally required for the student to learn from errors.

## 11. M2 feedback readiness considerations

M2 would require its own feedback identity.

Candidate M2 feedback readiness would require:

* feedback breve and completo aligned to M2-specific competencies and difficulty;
* no recycled M1 explanation under an M2 label;
* reasoning depth appropriate to M2 tasks;
* consistency across successive M2 loads;
* explanation of errors and correct reasoning in M2 terms;
* evidence that M2 feedback is not nominal or manually improvised as the normal path.

M2 candidate NO-GO if:

* M2 feedback is just M1 feedback renamed;
* M2 has no route or no evaluated questions;
* feedback does not match M2 difficulty;
* feedback requires ordinary human explanation to be pedagogically useful.

## 12. L1 feedback readiness considerations

L1 feedback must be reading-specific.

Candidate L1 feedback readiness would require:

* feedback breve and completo tied to texts and reading objectives;
* explanation of evidence in the text;
* explanation of inference, purpose, contextual vocabulary, structure, viewpoint, or other relevant reading skills when applicable;
* explanation of why the correct alternative follows from the text;
* explanation of why a distractor is tempting or incorrect when useful;
* student-friendly language that helps the student improve reading behavior;
* consistency across successive L1 loads.

L1 candidate NO-GO if:

* feedback is generic quiz feedback;
* feedback does not refer to text evidence;
* feedback ignores reading skill;
* feedback only states the correct alternative;
* L1 is nominal or lacks appropriate texts.

## 13. Pedagogically useful feedback definition

Pedagogically useful feedback should help the student answer:

* What happened?
* What idea matters?
* Why is the answer correct or incorrect?
* What should I review?
* How can I approach a similar exercise next time?
* When should I open the full explanation?

It should be:

* specific;
* accurate within the implemented scope;
* clear;
* actionable;
* connected to the question;
* aligned to the tutoria;
* safe for student confidence;
* honest about what it can and cannot infer.

## 14. Insufficient/superficial feedback definition

Feedback is insufficient or superficial if it:

* only says "correct" or "incorrect";
* only gives the right answer;
* is generic and not tied to the question;
* uses vague encouragement without learning signal;
* invents a diagnosis not supported by evidence;
* overclaims personalization;
* does not explain reasoning when reasoning is needed;
* does not support review of a wrong answer;
* does not help the student continue;
* requires ordinary human explanation to become useful;
* is inconsistent across tutorias or loads.

Superficial feedback would keep public Sales-Ready blocked.

## 15. Student experience boundary

The future student experience target is:

```text
I know whether my answer worked, I understand the central idea, I can open a fuller explanation when I need it, and I can keep studying without waiting for ordinary human explanation.
```

This is an internal experience target, not public copy.

This phase does not change what the student sees or can do.

It does not implement feedback breve or feedback completo.

It does not change `/now`, activity UI, dashboard, content, or runtime behavior.

## 16. Content/route implications

Two-state feedback readiness would imply future content and route work, but this phase does not implement it.

Future phases may need:

* item-level answer metadata;
* feedback breve content;
* feedback completo content;
* distractor explanations when useful;
* tutoria-specific feedback rules;
* L1 text-evidence references;
* M2-specific reasoning depth;
* M1 consistency across successive loads;
* admin/tutor visibility into feedback readiness;
* quality review and evidence collection.

Exact content formats, expert validation thresholds, and UI presentation remain future phase or Pro/high-level decisions.

## 17. Evidence required before public Sales-Ready

Future evidence would need to show:

* feedback breve exists for evaluated questions inside the promised M1/M2/L1 scope;
* feedback completo exists and is accessible;
* feedback is specific to each tutoria;
* M2 feedback is not recycled M1 feedback;
* L1 feedback is reading-specific and text-evidence aware;
* students can use feedback to understand mistakes or confirm reasoning;
* students can continue without ordinary human explanation;
* feedback is available across successive loads, not only a demo;
* feedback does not overpromise AI, personalization, score outcomes, or complete PAES coverage;
* actual app/student validation exists before any public Sales-Ready approval.

This phase provides no live validation.

## 19. FAIL criteria

This phase would fail if it:

* treats the candidate feedback boundary as final Sales-Ready approval;
* implies feedback is implemented;
* implies public launch or public sale;
* implies M1-only is Sales-Ready;
* claims an AI tutor exists;
* claims complete personalization;
* promises guaranteed learning or score improvement;
* claims complete PAES preparation;
* claims expert validation without evidence;
* authorizes content changes, UI changes, payment, trial activation, runtime enforcement, billing/subscription/payment, `/now`, student UI, admin mutation, DB, deploy, or code changes.

## 20. BLOCKED criteria

Future public Sales-Ready remains blocked if:

* feedback breve is missing;
* feedback completo is missing;
* feedback is generic answer-key feedback;
* M2 feedback is nominal or recycled from M1;
* L1 feedback is not reading-specific;
* feedback is inconsistent across successive loads;
* feedback requires ordinary human explanation to become useful;
* feedback UI is unavailable where needed;
* feedback evidence has not been validated with actual app/student flows;
* public copy overpromises feedback, AI tutor behavior, or guaranteed learning.

## 21. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation | Candidate future phase |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Two-state feedback sounds like an AI tutor | Creates unsupported public expectation. | State that feedback does not imply AI tutor or complete personalization. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1F` |
| Brief feedback becomes too shallow | Student sees correctness but cannot learn. | Require central idea and key error/concept signal. | Future feedback implementation/readiness phase |
| Complete feedback becomes a generic answer key | Does not support reasoning or transfer. | Require step-by-step reasoning and tutoria-specific explanation. | Future feedback implementation/readiness phase |
| M2 feedback is copied from M1 | M2 becomes nominal. | Require M2-specific depth, competencies, and difficulty. | Future M2 route/feedback readiness phase |
| L1 feedback is generic quiz feedback | Reading competence is not supported. | Require text evidence and reading-skill explanation. | Future L1 route/feedback readiness phase |
| Feedback is inconsistent across loads | Public continuity becomes unreliable. | Require evidence across successive loads. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1D` |
| Feedback overpromises improvement | Brand and ethical risk. | Keep improvement as internal evidence, not public promise. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1F` |
