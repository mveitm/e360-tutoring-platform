## 7. Feedback-readiness principles

Future L1 feedback for this set should be allowed only if it can:

* remain tied to the original Bexauri-created draft text;
* remain tied to `locating_information`;
* identify the explicit detail that supports the correct option;
* explain incorrect alternatives by clear textual reasons;
* avoid inference beyond the text;
* avoid external knowledge;
* avoid long quotation;
* avoid score, mastery, ranking, theta, PAES prediction, or guaranteed improvement claims;
* remain calm, concise, student-safe, and reading-specific.

Metadata notes and answer-key notes are not student-facing feedback.

## 8. Feedback breve readiness boundary

Future feedback breve should be able to:

* state whether the answer is correct or not, if a later implementation supports that state;
* point to the relevant explicit detail in a short way;
* avoid long quotation;
* avoid overexplaining;
* avoid score, mastery, ranking, theta, PAES prediction, guaranteed improvement, or complete-preparation claims;
* remain calm, student-safe, and reading-specific;
* avoid implying AI tutor behavior or hidden diagnostic certainty.

This document does not create feedback breve.

## 9. Feedback completo readiness boundary

Future feedback completo should be able to:

* explain why the correct answer is supported by one explicit detail;
* explain why each distractor fails for a clear textual reason;
* remain tied to locating information;
* avoid inference beyond the text;
* avoid overdiagnosing the student;
* avoid long quotation unless a later rights/editorial phase explicitly allows a short quotation style;
* stay concise enough for early L1 pilot use;
* remain student-safe and non-punitive.

This document does not create feedback completo.

## 10. Evidence-reference boundary

Future feedback should prefer pointing to or paraphrasing the relevant evidence rather than quoting.

Evidence-reference rules:

* Do not reproduce long text.
* Do not introduce facts outside the draft.
* Do not use official PAES/DEMRE text.
* Do not imply officialness or PAES-equivalence.
* Do not treat the original draft as approved product content.
* Keep evidence references short and tied to the question.

Because the source text is original Bexauri-created project content, future phases may decide whether short quotation is acceptable. That decision is not made here.

## 11. Distractor-explanation boundary

Future feedback may explain distractor failure only when each explanation can be tied to a clear textual reason.

Allowed future distractor-explanation behavior:

* identify that a distractor names a different time, object, container, place, role, or condition from the text;
* explain that the distractor is related to the text but does not answer the question asked;
* avoid introducing new facts;
* avoid calling the student's reasoning defective beyond what the answer choice supports.

Blocked distractor-explanation behavior:

* broad diagnosis of the student's ability;
* unsupported claims about attention, motivation, or reading level;
* explanations that require external context;
* explanations that quote large parts of the text;
* explanations that become new teaching content unrelated to locating explicit information.

## 15. Common blocked feedback patterns

Blocked feedback patterns for this set include:

* `This proves mastery.`
* `This predicts PAES performance.`
* `The app knows your reading level.`
* `You failed because...`
* broad psychological diagnosis;
* broad tutoring claims;
* explanations using information not in the text;
* feedback that requires quoting large parts of the text;
* feedback that treats metadata notes as student-facing copy;
* feedback that claims official PAES equivalence;
* feedback that implies complete L1 readiness.

Any future feedback using these patterns should be reworked or blocked before product consideration.

* imply the text or items are official PAES/DEMRE content;

## 21. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Feedback-readiness is mistaken for feedback creation | Would exceed phase scope | State no feedback copy, brief feedback, or complete feedback is created |
| Answer-key metadata becomes student-facing feedback | Could create weak or unsafe explanations | Require separate feedback authoring and review |
| Feedback overquotes the text | Creates poor style and possible rights drift | Prefer pointing/paraphrasing and avoid long quotation |
| Feedback overdiagnoses the student | Creates trust and safety risk | Limit feedback to answer/text relationship |
| Feedback claims mastery or PAES prediction | Creates unsupported commercial promise | Block score, mastery, theta, ranking, and prediction language |
| L1 readiness is inferred from one text and three items | Would weaken Sales-Ready gates | Keep L1 not ready and public Sales-Ready blocked |
