## 4. Purpose

The purpose is to classify the first three metadata-only concept records by relative risk and recommend whether each should be preserved, adjusted in a future metadata-revision phase, blocked, or prioritized for a later authorized drafting phase.

Any priority recommendation is a planning recommendation only. It does not authorize drafting now.

## 5. Review method

The review compares each record against the accepted L1 original-text chain:

* text-rights and editorial policy;
* text/editorial quality checklist;
* original-text authoring protocol;
* metadata schema;
* concept inventory template;
* first concept metadata records.

The review checks:

* rights/source risk;
* sensitivity risk;
* ambiguity risk;
* answerability risk;
* feedback feasibility;
* cognitive load;
* reading-skill clarity;
* whether the record remains metadata-only.

No web search was used. No official texts, released PAES items, copyrighted passages, news articles, blogs, commercial prep material, or AI-generated source claims were used.

## 7. Record 1 review: locating information

Record: `original_l1_concept_locating_information_01`.

Review classification:

* Preserve as metadata-only.
* Candidate for first future drafting priority, if a later phase explicitly authorizes drafting.
* No current block identified.

Reasoning:

* Rights/source risk appears low because the record is marked `original_bexauri_created_candidate`, with no official-source dependency, no copyrighted-source dependency, and no attribution requirement.
* Sensitivity risk appears low because sensitive topic flags are none and the future context is constrained as low-sensitivity.
* Ambiguity and answerability risk appear lower than the other records because the skill target is explicit information/evidence location.
* Cognitive load appears lower because the intended band is short, introductory, and low inference burden.
* Feedback feasibility appears comparatively safer because future feedback could point to evidence without requiring complex interpretation, if a future text is drafted cleanly.

Boundary:

* This record is not approved for drafting now.
* This record is not approved for student use or product use.
* This record does not create any text, question, distractor, feedback, StudyLoad, content registry entry, L1 readiness, or Sales-Ready approval.

## 8. Record 2 review: inference and relationships

Record: `original_l1_concept_inference_relationships_01`.

Review classification:

* Preserve as metadata-only.
* Useful future candidate, but not first priority unless future editorial direction wants moderate complexity.
* Recommend future metadata adjustment or tighter review before drafting.
* No current block identified.

Reasoning:

* Rights/source risk appears low for the same reason as Record 1: original candidate source, no official dependency, no copyrighted dependency, no attribution requirement.
* Sensitivity risk appears low, but future context still needs editorial confirmation.
* Ambiguity and answerability risk are moderate because supported inference and relationships can drift into multiple reasonable interpretations if the future text is not tightly controlled.
* Cognitive load is moderate because the record anticipates short-to-medium length, relationship cues, and moderate memory/inference burden.
* Feedback feasibility is plausible only if a future text contains clear relationship cues that can support correct-answer and distractor explanations without overquoting.

Future adjustment need:

* A later metadata-revision or drafting-prep phase should tighten expected relationship structure, answerability risk controls, evidence-cue requirements, and feedback-feasibility criteria before any drafting.

Boundary:

* This record is not approved for drafting now.
* This record is not approved for student use or product use.
* This record does not create any text, question, distractor, feedback, StudyLoad, content registry entry, L1 readiness, or Sales-Ready approval.

## 9. Record 3 review: purpose and viewpoint

Record: `original_l1_concept_purpose_viewpoint_01`.

Review classification:

* Preserve as metadata-only.
* Later-priority candidate.
* Recommend stronger future editorial review and likely future metadata adjustment before drafting.
* No current block identified.

Reasoning:

* Rights/source risk appears low because the record stays original-source, with no official or copyrighted dependency.
* Sensitivity risk is currently marked low, but purpose/viewpoint work can become sensitive if the future text uses controversial positions, cultural assumptions, or unclear speaker stance.

* Cognitive load is moderate because students may need to connect form, context, purpose, and position.

Future adjustment need:

* A later metadata-revision or drafting-prep phase should tighten sensitivity boundaries, stance/purpose evidence requirements, ambiguity controls, and editorial authority before any drafting.

Boundary:

* This record is not approved for drafting now.
* This record is not approved for student use or product use.
* This record does not create any text, question, distractor, feedback, StudyLoad, content registry entry, L1 readiness, or Sales-Ready approval.
