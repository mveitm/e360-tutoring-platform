## 4. Purpose

The purpose is to define what "indefinite operation" should mean for a future public Sales-Ready gate without overclaiming what Bexauri can do today.

This phase clarifies:

* what no ordinary functional wall should mean;
* what ordinary continuity should mean;
* what an acceptable stop state could be;
* what an unacceptable pause would be;
* what no ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory should mean;
* what evidence would be needed before public Sales-Ready;
* why this criterion does not mean infinite content, guaranteed improvement, complete PAES coverage, advanced adaptive AI, or unlimited human support.

## 5. Definition of "indefinite operation"

Candidate definition:

For a future public Sales-Ready gate, indefinite operation should mean that a student does not encounter an ordinary functional wall inside the promised tutoria scope while the subscription is active.

The app would need to provide one of the following without ordinary human intervention in the learning trajectory:

* a next pertinent StudyLoad or learning step;
* a clear and honest acceptable stop state;
* a supervised exception path that is rare, explicit, and not the normal way the student continues.

This would require:

* no ordinary pause caused by lack of next work;
* no ordinary human release step for trajectory continuation;
* no hidden manual technical setup as the normal path;
* no dependency on DB scripts, logs, or individual technical intervention for the student to keep moving;
* continuity inside the specific public promise made for M1, M2, and L1.

## 6. What "indefinite operation" does not mean

Indefinite operation does not mean:

* infinite content magic;
* guaranteed improvement;
* complete PAES preparation;
* adaptive AI, theta, mastery, or advanced personalization unless separately implemented and validated;
* unlimited human support;
* zero supervision, support, audit, or exception handling;
* no errors ever;
* no future content work;
* proof that Sales-Ready is achieved;
* proof that the public price, public promise, payment/subscription flow, or student learning value is validated.

The safe interpretation is narrower: no ordinary functional wall inside the promised scope while the subscription is active.

## 7. Shared cross-tutoria criteria

For each of M1, M2, and L1, a future public Sales-Ready gate would likely require:

* enrollment in the tutoria;
* a visible current or next learning step;
* successive loads or learning steps under the promised scope;
* a route identity specific to the tutoria;
* progression that is logical and pertinent, not random or merely repetitive;
* brief and complete feedback readiness, as a separate future gate;
* dashboard/homepage progress evidence readiness, as a separate future gate;
* return-and-resume continuity;
* no ordinary manual assignment;
* no hidden technical setup as normal operation;
* admin/tutor visibility without DB inspection as normal work;
* a support/failure path for exceptions;
* subscription/access alignment in future payment phases, without deciding payment here.

## 8. M1 indefinite operation criteria

M1 may have partial operational precedent, but that cannot be treated as public Sales-Ready.

Candidate M1 indefinite operation would require:

* M1 enrollment and a clear current M1 learning step;
* successive M1 loads beyond a short demo or fixed route fragment;
* logical progression through M1 skills and difficulty;
* ability to reinforce, bridge, advance, or pause explicitly without stranding the student;
* response to observed deficits through pertinent future loads;
* no ordinary functional wall while the subscription is active and the student remains inside the promised M1 scope;
* no ordinary human intervention needed for the M1 trajectory to continue;
* evidence that students can return and resume M1 work without manual technical setup.

M1 candidate NO-GO:

* finite demo route only;
* short route with no continuation plan;
* superficial feedback that does not support learning;
* continuation dependent on human/manual assignment;
* M1-only claim used as public Sales-Ready.

## 9. M2 indefinite operation criteria

M2 would require its own route/content identity before it can satisfy indefinite operation.

Candidate M2 indefinite operation would require:

* M2 enrollment and a clear current M2 learning step;
* M2-specific competencies, difficulty, and progression;
* successive M2 loads or learning steps;
* no duplication of M1 with only a changed label;
* continuity under the promised M2 scope while subscription is active;
* no ordinary human intervention needed for the M2 trajectory to continue;
* evidence that M2 is not nominal, empty, or manually assembled as the normal path.

M2 candidate NO-GO:

* nominal M2 label only;
* no M2 route;
* no M2 continuity;
* M2 content duplicated from M1 without its own purpose;
* ordinary human/manual intervention required for progress.

## 10. L1 indefinite operation criteria

L1 would require a reading-competence identity, not generic multiple-choice activity.

Candidate L1 indefinite operation would require:

* L1 enrollment and a clear current L1 learning step;
* appropriate texts;
* questions aligned to reading comprehension objectives;
* successive L1 loads or learning steps;
* progression across L1 reading skills such as inference, purpose, contextual vocabulary, structure, or other reading competencies;
* feedback that helps the student understand reading errors;
* continuity under the promised L1 scope while subscription is active;
* no ordinary human intervention needed for the L1 trajectory to continue.

L1 candidate NO-GO:

* generic quiz route;
* inadequate texts;
* insufficient reading-specific feedback;
* no L1 continuity;
* ordinary human/manual intervention required for progress.

## 11. Functional wall definition

A functional wall is a normal-path dead end that prevents the student from continuing inside the promised scope.

Candidate functional walls include:

* no next load or learning step when one is promised;
* no clear explanation of what the student should do next;
* no acceptable stop state;
* no dashboard/homepage signal of progress or next work;
* access state and runtime behavior disagreeing;
* payment/subscription state implying access that the app cannot sustain;
* M2 or L1 existing only as labels;
* ordinary need for human/manual assignment;
* ordinary need for DB scripts, logs, or technical operator intervention;
* route exhaustion hidden from the student;
* content gaps masked by manual tutoring.

## 12. Acceptable stop states

An acceptable stop state is not a functional wall if it is honest, planned, and compatible with the public promise.

Candidate acceptable stop states:

* planned session completion with a visible next available step later;
* milestone review that is explicitly described and does not hide missing ordinary content;
* transparent pause for a supervised exception;
* subscription expiry, cancellation, or pause where future payment/access policy explicitly supports it;
* completion of a clearly limited scope that was stated before use.

For public Sales-Ready, an acceptable stop state must not contradict the subscription promise.

If the public promise says the student can continue while subscribed, then repeated "no content available" states are functional walls, not acceptable stop states.

## 13. Unacceptable pauses

Unacceptable pauses would include:

* waiting for a human to manually decide the next ordinary StudyLoad;
* waiting for a technical operator to create or assign work as the normal path;
* waiting because the route has no next content;
* waiting because M2 or L1 are nominal;
* waiting because payment/access/subscription state is incoherent;
* waiting because dashboard progress or next-step state is missing;
* waiting because feedback is unavailable in a way that blocks learning continuity;
* waiting because hidden admin or DB work is required.

These pauses would keep public Sales-Ready blocked.

## 14. No-human-intervention trajectory boundary

"No ordinary human intervention" means no ordinary intervention in the learning trajectory.

The human may still:

* supervise;
* audit;
* support exceptions;
* review quality globally;
* improve content and product based on evidence;
* handle support and escalation.

The human must not be:

* the ordinary mechanism that releases each next step;
* the hidden manual tutor that makes the app appear complete;
* the person who compensates for missing M2/L1 route identity;
* the technical operator required for normal progress;
* the source of unsupported public promises.

## 15. Route/content implications

Indefinite operation would imply future route/content work, but this phase does not implement it.

Future M1/M2/L1 route/content readiness would likely need:

* enough initial and successive loads inside the promised scope;
* reinforcement paths;
* bridge paths;
* advance paths;
* explicit milestone or review states;
* content identity by tutoria;
* a way to avoid random repetition;
* a way to avoid premature dead ends;
* evidence that the app can continue without hidden manual assembly.

The exact number of loads, progression model, and acceptable scope boundaries remain Pro/high-level or future phase decisions.

## 16. Evidence required before public Sales-Ready

Future evidence would need to show, at minimum:

* M1, M2, and L1 each have a real route/content identity.
* A student can enroll in each tutoria under the future approved flow.
* Each tutoria can provide successive pertinent loads or steps.
* Students can return and resume.
* No ordinary functional wall appears inside the promised scope.
* No ordinary human intervention is required in the learning trajectory.
* Admin/tutor can observe progress without DB/scripts/logs as the normal view.
* Brief and complete feedback readiness is satisfied under a future feedback gate.
* Dashboard/homepage progress evidence is satisfied under a future progress gate.
* Payment/subscription/access alignment is verified under future payment/access phases if public sale is involved.
* Evidence comes from actual app/student validation, not document-only reasoning.

This phase provides no such live validation.

## 17. PASS criteria

Documentation PASS for this phase requires:

* candidate definition of indefinite operation documented;
* what it does and does not mean documented;
* shared M1/M2/L1 criteria documented;
* functional wall, acceptable stop state, unacceptable pause, and no-human-intervention boundaries documented;
* evidence needs documented;
* public Sales-Ready kept blocked;
* no implementation or operational change performed.

Future public Sales-Ready PASS would require separate evidence that M1, M2, and L1 meet the approved criterion in real flows.

## 18. FAIL criteria

This phase would fail if it:

* treats the candidate criterion as final Sales-Ready approval;
* implies public launch or public sale;
* implies M1-only is Sales-Ready;
* promises infinite content;
* promises guaranteed improvement;
* promises complete PAES preparation;
* claims adaptive AI, theta, mastery, or advanced personalization without implementation and validation;
* authorizes payment, trial activation, runtime enforcement, billing/subscription/payment, `/now`, student UI, admin mutation, DB, deploy, or code changes.

## 19. BLOCKED criteria

Future public Sales-Ready remains blocked if:

* M2 route/content identity is missing;
* L1 route/content identity is missing;
* M1 is only a finite demo or short route;
* brief and complete feedback are not ready;
* dashboard/homepage progress evidence is not ready;
* students encounter ordinary functional walls;
* ordinary human intervention is needed in the learning trajectory;
* payment/subscription/access state is incoherent;
* public copy overpromises;
* compact terms, support, pause, cancellation, or expiry boundaries are missing where relevant.

## 20. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation | Candidate future phase |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| "Indefinite" sounds like infinite content | Creates impossible public expectation. | Define it as no ordinary functional wall inside promised scope. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1F` |
| M1 partial precedent is mistaken for Sales-Ready | M1-only cannot satisfy public M1/M2/L1 gate. | Keep M1-only blocked for public Sales-Ready. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1D` |
| M2 or L1 become nominal labels | Public promise would exceed product reality. | Require route/content identity and evidence per tutoria. | Future M2/L1 route readiness phase |
| Human support hides product gaps | Creates false continuity signal. | Require no ordinary human intervention in trajectory and record exceptions. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1E` |
| Payment implies continuity the app cannot provide | Commercial trust risk. | Keep payment/subscription readiness separate and gated. | Future payment/subscription readiness phase |
| Dashboard does not show progress or next step | Student may feel stranded even if content exists. | Define progress evidence requirements separately. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1C` |
| Feedback does not support learning | Continuity without learning value is not sufficient. | Define two-state feedback boundary separately. | `MVP-COMMERCIAL-SALES-READY-GATE-1B` |
