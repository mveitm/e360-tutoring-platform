## 3. Purpose

MVP-CONTENT-1 defines the first skeleton of the PAES_M1 master roadmap.

This is not a fixed four-week plan.

This is not a micro-roadmap.

This is not an exercise bank.

This is the master pedagogical structure from which Bexauri can later extract active slices and micro StudyLoads.

## 4. Source basis

Primary source basis:

- DEMRE PAES Regular M1 - Admisión 2027 official temario.
- PAES M1 evaluates mathematical competence through four skills:
  - Resolver problemas.
  - Modelar.
  - Representar.
  - Argumentar.
- PAES M1 organizes knowledge from 7th grade to 2nd grade high school into four axes:
  - Números.
  - Álgebra y funciones.
  - Geometría.
  - Probabilidad y estadística.

This skeleton is a Bexauri pedagogical organization derived from the official PAES M1 structure.

## 5. Relationship with the SRA

The roadmap must follow the dynamic SRA architecture:

master roadmap
-> active slice
-> micro StudyLoad
-> evidence
-> supervised adjustment
-> next active slice

The master roadmap contains the complete structure.

The active slice is the next useful segment for a specific student.

The micro StudyLoad is the immediate learning action.

The system must not force every student through the same calendar or the same pace.

## 6. Master roadmap principles

The PAES_M1 master roadmap should:

- cover the official PAES M1 axes;
- organize skills and subskills pedagogically;
- expose prerequisite relationships;
- support different student rhythms;
- allow fast progress when evidence supports it;
- allow reinforcement when evidence shows fragility;
- support human supervision;
- support later automation without requiring it now;
- avoid claiming PAES score prediction from micro work;
- avoid automatic mastery decisions in MVP-Beta.

## 7. Difficulty bands

Bexauri will use practical difficulty bands before any formal adaptive model exists.

### D0 - Access / readiness

Purpose:

- reduce entry friction;
- confirm very basic prerequisite recognition;
- detect severe gaps without intimidating the student.

Evidence examples:

- identifies basic operation;
- recognizes simple algebraic structure;
- chooses correct representation when obvious.

### D1 - Foundational

Purpose:

- build reliable base;
- practice direct application;
- identify misconceptions.

Evidence examples:

- solves routine problems;
- applies formulas or rules in simple contexts;
- makes few procedural errors.

### D2 - Consolidation

Purpose:

- connect procedures with meaning;
- combine two or more steps;
- increase transfer.

Evidence examples:

- chooses the right strategy;
- interprets information;
- moves between representations;
- explains or justifies simple reasoning.

### D3 - PAES-oriented

Purpose:

- simulate common PAES complexity;
- integrate context, representation, and decision-making.

Evidence examples:

- solves contextual problems;
- models a situation;
- interprets graph/table/expression;
- distinguishes distractors.

### D4 - Challenge / validation

Purpose:

- validate fluency and readiness under higher pressure;
- identify whether the student can accelerate.

Evidence examples:

- solves unfamiliar or multi-step problems;
- shows fluency;
- maintains accuracy with less scaffolding.

## 14. Active slice extraction rules

A future active slice should be extracted using evidence and supervision.

Initial MVP-Beta manual rules:

### Advance

Consider advancing when:

- student answers most items correctly;
- self-report is positive or neutral;
- errors are not repeated;
- the skill is not a critical prerequisite requiring more validation.

### Reinforce

Consider reinforcement when:

- repeated error appears;
- student self-reports difficulty;
- prerequisite weakness is visible;
- correct answers appear slow, uncertain, or inconsistent.

### Validate

Consider a challenge or validation item when:

- student performs strongly at current band;
- evidence suggests the student may be ready to skip ahead;
- supervisor wants to confirm fluency.

### Hold

Consider holding current band when:

- evidence is mixed;
- student self-report conflicts with answer evidence;
- more data is needed.

### Redirect

Consider redirecting when:

- student is working on the wrong prerequisite;
- error suggests a deeper gap;
- current active slice is not pedagogically useful.
