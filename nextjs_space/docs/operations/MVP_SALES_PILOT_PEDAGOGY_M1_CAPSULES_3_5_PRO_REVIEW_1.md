## 8. Difficulty and reading-load review

Capsule 3 difficulty is appropriate for early M1 calibration. The everyday contexts are short, readable, and suitable for 1-3 closed MVP students. The wording revisions should reduce ambiguity without increasing reading load.

Capsule 5 difficulty is also appropriate. The only reading-load concern is item 2: an L-shaped room can be hard to visualize if no diagram is present. The next revision should either clarify the verbal decomposition or mark a simple diagram requirement.

Neither capsule should be made harder in the revision phase. The aim is clarity, not added complexity.

## 9. Distractor review

Capsule 3 distractors are interpretable:

- wrong base or percent interpretation;
- reporting the decrease instead of the remaining amount;
- comparing total price or quantity rather than unit price;
- additive or excessive multiplicative reasoning in ratio.

Capsule 5 distractors are interpretable:

- area/perimeter confusion;
- adding dimensions instead of computing areas;
- unit conversion errors;
- irrelevant dimension or formula choice.

The distractors are acceptable for source-authoring review. Final distractor rationales still need later ficha and feedback-contract treatment.
