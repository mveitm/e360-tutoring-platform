# BPTT — Addendum de mejora continua pedagogica supervisada

**Phase:** `MVP-SALES-PILOT-PEDAGOGY-BPTT-CONTINUOUS-IMPROVEMENT-ADDENDUM-1`
**Nature:** Transversal BPTT pedagogical governance addendum.
**Scope:** Documentation only. No implementation.

## 1. Executive verdict

```text
BPTT_CONTINUOUS_IMPROVEMENT_PRINCIPLE_DOCUMENTED
```

Continuous improvement is a transversal Bexauri principle.

Bexauri does not merely deliver learning activities. Bexauri improves the quality of its capsules, feedback, step-by-step help, routes, evidence interpretation, supervision loops, and content bank quality over time, based on evidence of use, under explicit governance.

This addendum does not authorize implementation, generated capsules, API integration, automatic route mutation, content registry changes, continuity logic changes, adaptive AI claims, mastery claims, theta claims, PAES score claims, readiness claims, or Sales-Ready claims.

## 2. Purpose of this addendum

This addendum elevates continuous improvement from the M1/API capsule generation strategy into BPTT as a transversal pedagogical/tactical principle for Bexauri tutorias.

The purpose is to define how Bexauri may improve its pedagogical layer over time without pretending that the system is autonomous, adaptive, diagnostic, psychometric, or self-changing.

It applies beyond PAES_M1 and beyond API generation. It governs future thinking about:

- capsules;
- feedback;
- step-by-step help;
- routes;
- evidence interpretation;
- supervision loops;
- content bank quality;
- generated content contracts;
- validation criteria.

## 3. Why continuous improvement belongs in BPTT

BPTT defines the transversal grammar of a Bexauri tutoria: purpose, domain, progression, resources, capsules, evidence, feedback, self-report, continuity, progress, and review.

Continuous improvement belongs in BPTT because those elements are not static. A tutoring system should learn operationally from evidence of use:

- which capsule wording creates confusion;
- which distractors reveal useful patterns;
- which feedback supports recovery;
- which step-by-step help is overused or insufficient;
- which route creates tunnel risk;
- which generated candidates fail validation;
- which banked capsules are worth reusing, revising, or retiring.

Without a transversal improvement principle, Bexauri risks treating every capsule as a one-time artifact and every route as a fixed sequence. With this addendum, improvement becomes governed pedagogy, not accidental iteration.

## 4. Definition of continuous improvement

Continuous improvement means a governed, supervised, versioned process through which Bexauri uses evidence of use and quality review to improve:

- capsule wording;
- item quality;
- answer options;
- distractor design;
- feedback breve;
- feedback completo;
- step-by-step help;
- metadata;
- difficulty calibration;
- capsule order;
- route recommendations;
- evidence interpretation;
- supervision loops;
- source packet quality;
- validation criteria;
- living capsule bank quality.

The process is evidence-informed and conservative. It should improve the pedagogical artifacts and decision rules that Bexauri controls, while respecting inference limits.

## 5. What continuous improvement is not

Continuous improvement does not mean:

- autonomous adaptive AI;
- mastery;
- theta;
- PAES score prediction;
- diagnostic certainty;
- readiness certainty;
- personalized route certainty;
- unsupervised self-changing pedagogy;
- automatic route mutation;
- automatic promotion of generated content;
- automatic retirement of capsules;
- a claim that the system learns by itself;
- a claim that Bexauri knows exactly what the student needs.

Continuous improvement is not a substitute for BPCPI, BPTT, roadmap governance, capsule fichas, feedback contracts, validation contracts, or human review.

## 6. Relationship to BPCPI

BPCPI requires learning to be guided, progressive, evidentiable, revisable, and honest about inference limits.

This addendum extends that requirement over time. If Bexauri observes that a capsule, feedback message, step-by-step flow, or route decision does not support learning well enough, Bexauri should improve the artifact or policy through a governed process.

Continuous improvement therefore supports BPCPI by strengthening:

- purposeful practice;
- interpretable evidence;
- actionable feedback;
- revisable learning;
- honest continuity;
- limits against pseudo-intelligence.

It does not weaken BPCPI limits. Weak evidence remains weak evidence even if accumulated in an improvement workflow.

## 7. Relationship to roadmap maestro and microroadmap vivo

The BPTT roadmap/microroadmap contract distinguishes:

- simple sequence;
- roadmap maestro;
- microroadmap vivo del estudiante.

Continuous improvement applies to each level, with different authority:

- A simple sequence can be improved when evidence shows confusion, monotony, or bad order.
- A roadmap maestro can be improved when coverage, progression, or node definitions are weak.
- A microroadmap hypothesis can be improved when evidence interpretation, route explanation, or supervision rules are insufficient.

This does not mean automatic route mutation. Route policy changes require governance, documentation, and, when material, human review.

## 8. Relationship to capsules

Capsules are not one-time exercises. They are pedagogical units that should become clearer, more useful, and better governed over time.

Continuous improvement can affect:

- capsule purpose;
- wording;
- item selection;
- item count;
- distractors;
- feedback;
- step-by-step help;
- metadata;
- difficulty;
- reuse status;
- retirement status.

A capsule can be improved only through a documented/versioned change path. A future system may suggest improvements, but suggestion does not equal approval.

## 9. Relationship to feedback and step-by-step help

Feedback and step-by-step help are central improvement targets.

Bexauri should monitor whether feedback and help actually support recovery:

- Does the student correct the misconception later?
- Does the same distractor repeat?
- Does the student say "me costo" despite answering correctly?
- Does the student use step-by-step repeatedly without improvement?
- Does feedback remain too generic?
- Does full feedback explain the reasoning, not just the answer?

Feedback and step-by-step help must be improved under supervision and versioning. This addendum does not authorize writing feedback into code or registry.

## 10. Relationship to evidence and student performance

Continuous improvement is based on evidence of use, not on intuition alone.

Relevant evidence may include:

- selected answers;
- repeated wrong distractor patterns;
- correctness patterns;
- mismatch between correctness and self-report;
- high "me costo" despite correct answer;
- high confidence with wrong answer;
- frequent step-by-step use;
- abandonment or non-completion;
- repeated low performance on same node;
- feedback that fails to support recovery;
- ambiguity detected by student or supervisor;
- human tutor/admin quality flags.

Evidence must be interpreted cautiously. Evidence can trigger review; it does not by itself authorize strong claims.

## 11. Relationship to human supervision

Human supervision is the governance layer that prevents continuous improvement from becoming unsupervised self-changing pedagogy.

Human supervision should focus on:

- performance patterns;
- feedback quality;
- step-by-step quality;
- anomalies;
- repeated validation failures;
- capsule promotion/retirement;
- route policy changes;
- high-risk language;
- improvement priorities;
- cost/reuse balance.

Human supervision does not need to block every ordinary student action, but it must govern improvement decisions that change pedagogical quality, policy, claims, or route behavior.

## 12. Relationship to living capsule bank

The living capsule bank is the reusable memory of approved capsule quality.

Continuous improvement feeds the bank by deciding whether a capsule should be:

- drafted;
- validated;
- reused;
- revised;
- promoted;
- monitored;
- retired;
- replaced;
- split;
- merged;
- held for human review.

Bank quality should improve over time through evidence and versioning. The bank should not become an unreviewed pile of generated or historical capsules.

## 13. Relationship to generated content

The M1/API strategy established that future API generation should produce complete pedagogical capsules, not exercises only.

This addendum generalizes the improvement principle:

- generated content can be an input to improvement;
- validation failures can trigger contract improvements;
- generated capsule performance can inform bank quality;
- generated capsule families require approval before broad use;
- repeated generation should be avoided when reuse is pedagogically sufficient.

This addendum does not authorize API integration, external API calls, generated capsules, generated content assignment, schema changes, registry changes, or runtime validation logic.

## 14. Transversal applicability across tutorias

Continuous improvement applies across Bexauri tutorias:

- PAES_M1;
- PAES_M2;
- PAES Competencia Lectora;
- future test-oriented tutorias;
- future skill-oriented tutorias;
- writing, reading, reasoning, study habits, and other skill domains.

Each tutoria must adapt the principle to its domain. Test-oriented tutorias may use official axes, item types, and coverage constraints. Skill-oriented tutorias may use rubrics, productions, revisions, transfer, and autonomy evidence.

The principle is transversal, but implementation details must be derived per tutoria.

## 15. Governance principles

Continuous improvement must be:

- supervised;
- governed;
- versioned;
- evidence-based;
- documented;
- auditable;
- conservative about inference;
- compatible with BPCPI and BPTT;
- specific to the relevant tutoria contract;
- safe against pseudo-adaptive claims.

Improvement decisions should document:

- signal observed;
- affected artifact or policy;
- proposed change;
- evidence strength;
- expected benefit;
- risks;
- reviewer or governance path;
- version impact;
- rollback or retirement path when relevant.

## 16. Signals that may trigger improvement

Signals may include:

- repeated wrong distractor patterns;
- mismatch between correctness and self-report;
- high "me costo" despite correct answer;
- high confidence with wrong answer;
- frequent step-by-step use;
- abandonment or non-completion;
- repeated low performance on same node;
- feedback that fails to support recovery;
- ambiguity detected by student or supervisor;
- human tutor/admin quality flags;
- generated capsule validation failures;
- overuse of one axis or tunnel risk;
- monotony risk;
- route imbalance;
- cost inefficiency through excessive generation.

These signals trigger review or improvement work. They do not automatically prove mastery, diagnosis, readiness, or route certainty.

## 17. What can be improved

Bexauri may improve, under the correct future phase and governance:

- capsule wording;
- item quality;
- answer options;
- distractor design;
- feedback breve;
- feedback completo;
- step-by-step help;
- metadata;
- difficulty calibration;
- capsule order;
- reuse/retirement status;
- route recommendations;
- source packet quality;
- validation criteria.

This addendum defines the principle. It does not perform any of those improvements.

## 18. What requires human review

Human review is required for:

- retiring a capsule;
- promoting a capsule to trusted/reusable;
- changing core pedagogical criteria;
- changing route policy;
- approving a new generated capsule family;
- accepting repeated anomalies as valid pattern;
- high-risk student-facing feedback language;
- claims about readiness, mastery, score or diagnosis.

Future automation may assist review, but it must not silently approve these decisions without the required governance path.

## 19. Prohibited claims

Continuous improvement does not authorize claims of:

- adaptive AI;
- mastery;
- theta;
- PAES score;
- diagnostic certainty;
- readiness certainty;
- personalized route certainty;
- autonomous pedagogy;
- unsupervised learning by the system.

Allowed language should remain cautious:

```text
The evidence suggests...
This pattern may indicate...
This capsule may need review...
This route policy should be reviewed...
More evidence is needed before a stronger claim.
```

## 20. Documentation and versioning expectations

Continuous improvement must leave a trace.

Expected documentation/versioning artifacts may include:

- improvement logs;
- capsule version notes;
- source packet revisions;
- feedback contract revisions;

- human review decisions;
- known issue records;

Future `PHASE_LOG.md` entries should stay compact and point to dedicated documents. Long-form improvement evidence must live under `nextjs_space/docs/operations/`.
