### Capsule 3 item 1 — Percent of a quantity

Draft stem:

```text
En un curso hay 40 estudiantes. El 25% participa en el taller de ciencias. ¿Cuántos estudiantes participan en ese taller?
```

Answer options:

| Option | Text |
|---|---|
| A | 8 |
| B | 10 |
| C | 15 |
| D | 25 |

Proposed correct option:

```text
B
```

Short rationale:

```text
25% equivale a un cuarto. Un cuarto de 40 es 10.
```

Expected evidence:

The item may suggest whether the student identifies the whole quantity and can interpret 25% as a proportional part of that whole.

Feedback requirement:

Future feedback should name the base quantity, connect 25% with one fourth, and distinguish percent value from number of students.

Step-by-step requirement:

Identify the total, translate 25% to 1/4 or 0.25, calculate the part, and check that the answer is a count of students.

Inference limit:

One response may suggest a percent-of-quantity signal only. More evidence is needed before any stronger claim about Numbers or proportional reasoning.

### Capsule 3 item 2 — Percentage change with base quantity

Draft stem:

```text
Una botella tenía 600 ml de agua. Después de beber, queda un 20% menos que al inicio. ¿Cuánta agua queda en la botella?
```

Answer options:

| Option | Text |
|---|---|
| A | 120 ml |
| B | 480 ml |
| C | 580 ml |
| D | 720 ml |

Proposed correct option:

```text
B
```

Short rationale:

```text
20% de 600 ml es 120 ml. Si queda 20% menos, se resta 120 a 600: quedan 480 ml.
```

Expected evidence:

The item may suggest whether the student uses the original amount as base and interprets a decrease as subtraction from that base.

Feedback requirement:

Future feedback should clarify the base quantity, explain the meaning of "20% menos", and separate calculating the decrease from reporting the remaining amount.

Step-by-step requirement:

Identify the initial amount, calculate 20% of that amount, decide whether the context asks for the decrease or the remaining amount, and verify units.

Inference limit:

A wrong response may indicate base confusion, operation confusion, or reading difficulty. It should not be treated as a diagnosis.

### Capsule 3 item 3 — Unit price / proportional comparison

Draft stem:

```text
En una tienda, 3 cuadernos cuestan $2.400. En otra tienda, 5 cuadernos cuestan $4.500. Si los cuadernos son iguales, ¿en qué tienda conviene comprar si se busca el menor precio por cuaderno?
```

Answer options:

| Option | Text |
|---|---|
| A | En la primera tienda, porque cada cuaderno cuesta $800. |
| B | En la segunda tienda, porque cada cuaderno cuesta $900. |
| C | En la primera tienda, porque 3 cuadernos son menos que 5. |
| D | En la segunda tienda, porque el total es mayor. |

Proposed correct option:

```text
A
```

Short rationale:

```text
Primera tienda: 2400 / 3 = 800 por cuaderno. Segunda tienda: 4500 / 5 = 900 por cuaderno. Conviene el menor precio unitario.
```

Expected evidence:

The item may suggest whether the student compares proportional quantities by unit price rather than by total price or item count.

Feedback requirement:

Future feedback should explain why comparing totals is not enough and why price per unit is the relevant relation.

Step-by-step requirement:

Compute each unit price, compare the unit prices, and interpret which option is more convenient in context.

Inference limit:

This item gives one signal about proportional comparison. It does not establish general performance on ratios or financial contexts.

### Capsule 3 item 4 — Ratio/equivalence with reasonableness check

Draft stem:

```text
Para preparar jugo, una receta usa 2 vasos de concentrado por cada 5 vasos de agua. Si se usan 4 vasos de concentrado y se mantiene la misma proporción, ¿cuántos vasos de agua se necesitan?
```

Answer options:

| Option | Text |
|---|---|
| A | 7 |
| B | 8 |
| C | 10 |
| D | 20 |

Proposed correct option:

```text
C
```

Short rationale:

```text
Al duplicar el concentrado de 2 a 4 vasos, también se duplica el agua de 5 a 10 vasos para mantener la proporción.
```

Expected evidence:

The item may suggest whether the student preserves a proportional relation when both quantities must scale together.

Feedback requirement:

Future feedback should show that the relation is multiplicative and that both quantities change by the same factor.

Step-by-step requirement:

Identify the original ratio, determine the scale factor, apply it to the water quantity, and check whether the mixture stays proportional.

Inference limit:

A distractor may indicate additive reasoning or over-multiplication, but a single item is only an early signal.

### Capsule 5 item 1 — Area vs perimeter distinction

```text
Un jardín rectangular mide 6 m de largo y 4 m de ancho. Se quiere poner una reja alrededor de todo el borde. ¿Qué medida se necesita calcular?
```

| Option | Text |
|---|---|
| A | El área: 6 × 4 |
| B | El perímetro: 6 + 4 + 6 + 4 |
| C | La diagonal: 6 + 4 |
| D | El volumen: 6 × 4 × 2 |

Proposed correct option:

```text
B
```

Short rationale:

```text
Una reja alrededor del borde requiere la suma de los lados, es decir, el perímetro.
```

Expected evidence:

The item may suggest whether the student distinguishes the requested magnitude from the dimensions given.

Feedback requirement:

Future feedback should contrast area and perimeter through the context: covering surface versus surrounding a border.

Step-by-step requirement:

Identify what the context asks, decide whether it refers to border or surface, select the relevant relation, and check units.

Inference limit:

This item gives one signal about area/perimeter distinction. It does not establish Geometry performance.

```text

```

Answer options:

| Option | Text |
|---|---|

| B | 23 m² |
| C | 30 m² |
| D | 40 m² |

```text
B
```

### Capsule 5 item 4 — Area comparison between rectangles

Draft stem:

```text
Dos zonas rectangulares se quieren cubrir con pasto. La zona A mide 4 m de largo y 3 m de ancho. La zona B mide 5 m de largo y 2 m de ancho. ¿Cuál zona tiene mayor superficie?
```

Answer options:

| Option | Text |
|---|---|
| A | La zona A, porque su área es 12 m². |
| B | La zona B, porque su área es 10 m². |
| C | Las dos zonas tienen la misma área. |
| D | No se puede saber sin calcular el perímetro. |

```text
A
```


Expected evidence:

Feedback requirement:

Future feedback should connect "mayor superficie" with area calculation and distinguish it from comparing only one side length or perimeter.

Step-by-step requirement:

Identify the two rectangles, calculate each area, compare the two results, and check square units.

Inference limit:

One response may suggest an area-comparison signal only. It cannot establish broader Geometry understanding.
