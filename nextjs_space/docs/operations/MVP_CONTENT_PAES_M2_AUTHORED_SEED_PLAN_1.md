## M2 seed principles

The M2 seed must be small, authored, QA-able and representative. It should demonstrate that Bexauri can support PAES_M2 without implying full PAES coverage.

Seed principles:

- start with 4 candidate capsules for first closed-pilot exposure planning, extendable to 8 later;
- keep every M2 capsule to 3-4 exercises, maximum 4;
- begin with one low-risk text-first capsule before visual-heavy items;
- include at least one data/probability/statistics capsule;
- classify visual/math stimulus requirements before implementation;
- avoid figure-heavy geometry until deterministic figure or approved asset workflow is ready;
- include brief and complete authoredFeedback in every implemented item;
- preserve no Sales-Ready, M2-ready, mastery, theta, PAES score, adaptive AI or route-certainty claim.

## Feedback requirements

Every M2 seed item must include:

- `authoredFeedback.briefId`;
- `authoredFeedback.completeId`;
- `authoredFeedback.version`;
- `authoredFeedback.brief`;
- `authoredFeedback.complete`.

Brief feedback must:

- be student-facing and concise;
- name the key mathematical idea;
- point to the first correction or review focus;
- avoid key-check-only language;
- avoid internal metadata and readiness/agentic claims.

Complete feedback must:

- restate what was being asked;
- identify the relevant data, variables, relation, formula or table/graph element;
- solve step by step;
- justify the correct option;
- explain a likely error or distractor;
- reference the same visual/table/math stimulus shown to the student when applicable;
- remain readable on mobile;
- avoid leakage, Sales-Ready, mastery, theta, score, adaptive AI or route certainty claims.

## Deferred M2 content

Deferred until visual/rendering or pedagogy readiness:

- geometry with required figures;
- graph interpretation where graph reading is central;
- non-linear function graphs;
- notation-heavy systems if plain text is ambiguous;
- any item that requires final student-facing AI-generated image/figure;
- broad PAES_M2 coverage claims;
- adaptive/agentic recommendation or generation.
